A glacier flows from land to the sea
…and attacks the  front of the glacier
...until it reaches a point, beyond which it cannot spread any further
...where a smaller part of the glacier is vulnerable to ice loss...
Warm water flows into deep fjords...
Icebergs calve away and the glacier retreats
When the front of the glacier meets the sea, it is called a marine-terminating glacier
...or further inland...
...where more of the glacier is exposed to thinning and more ice may be lost to the sea
...and the thickness of the ice...
The glacier becomes thinner as a result
...this limit can be close to the front of the glacier...
Depending on the shape of the land underneath the glacier...
And this thinning spreads throughout the glacier...
